# Course Outline: Biblical Foundations I
## Bible Course App

**Course ID:** `biblical_foundations_01`  
**Total Lessons:** 24  
**Duration:** 3–4 weeks (daily study)

---

## Course Overview

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this course, students will:
1. Identify major covenants structuring biblical narrative
2. Explain the relationship between Old and New Testament
3. Describe basic genre categories and their reading strategies
4. Trace key theological themes across Scripture
5. Apply historical-grammatical method to selected passages

### Theological Framework
- **Tradition:** Evangelical Reformed
- **Hermeneutic:** Historical-grammatical
- **Approach:** Canonical-theological
- **Emphasis:** Covenant structure as organizing principle

---

## Unit 1: Introduction to Biblical Study (3 lessons)

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Biblical Study
- **Objective:** Understand the Bible as a library of texts requiring method
- **Passages:** None (introductory)
- **Key Terms:** Canon, genre

### Lesson 2: Historical-Grammatical Method
- **Objective:** Explain the interpretive approach used throughout this course
- **Passages:** None (methodological)
- **Key Terms:** Historical-grammatical method, exegesis

### Lesson 3: Covenant in Genesis
- **Objective:** Identify covenant as a structuring theme in Genesis
- **Passages:** Genesis 12:1-3, Genesis 15:1-6
- **Key Terms:** Covenant, unconditional promise

---

## Unit 2: Covenant Framework in Genesis (5 lessons)

### Lesson 4: Creation and Commission
- **Objective:** Recognize creation as foundational covenant context
- **Passages:** Genesis 1:26-28, Genesis 2:15-17
- **Key Terms:** Image of God, dominion mandate

### Lesson 5: Fall and Consequence
- **Objective:** Trace the narrative movement from creation to judgment
- **Passages:** Genesis 3:1-7, Genesis 3:14-19
- **Key Terms:** Fall, covenant curse

### Lesson 6: Noah and Universal Covenant
- **Objective:** Identify covenant preservation themes in the flood narrative
- **Passages:** Genesis 6:17-22, Genesis 9:8-17
- **Key Terms:** Noahic covenant, universal promise

### Lesson 7: Abraham and Promise
- **Objective:** Explain the structure of Abrahamic covenant promises
- **Passages:** Genesis 15:1-6, Genesis 17:1-8
- **Key Terms:** Divine election, faith

### Lesson 8: Testing and Confirmation
- **Objective:** Analyze covenant testing in the binding of Isaac
- **Passages:** Genesis 22:1-18
- **Key Terms:** Covenant oath, testing

---

## Unit 3: Exodus and Law (6 lessons)

### Lesson 9: Oppression and Deliverance
- **Objective:** Connect exodus event to covenant faithfulness
- **Passages:** Exodus 2:23-25, Exodus 6:2-8
- **Key Terms:** Redemption, covenant memory

### Lesson 10: Passover and Substitution
- **Objective:** Identify substitutionary themes in Passover narrative
- **Passages:** Exodus 12:1-13, Exodus 12:21-28
- **Key Terms:** Passover, substitutionary death

### Lesson 11: Sinai Covenant Structure
- **Objective:** Recognize covenant treaty form in Exodus 19-20
- **Passages:** Exodus 19:3-6, Exodus 20:1-17
- **Key Terms:** Sinai covenant, Decalogue

### Lesson 12: Law and Covenant Relationship
- **Objective:** Explain the function of law within covenant structure
- **Passages:** Exodus 24:3-8, Deuteronomy 6:1-9
- **Key Terms:** Covenant stipulations, obedience

### Lesson 13: Tabernacle and Presence
- **Objective:** Describe tabernacle as covenant meeting place
- **Passages:** Exodus 25:1-9, Exodus 40:34-38
- **Key Terms:** Tabernacle, divine presence

### Lesson 14: Sacrifice and Atonement
- **Objective:** Identify basic sacrificial categories and purposes
- **Passages:** Leviticus 1:1-9, Leviticus 16:15-22
- **Key Terms:** Atonement, Day of Atonement

---

## Unit 4: Land and Kingship (6 lessons)

### Lesson 15: Conquest and Covenant Fulfillment
- **Objective:** Trace land promise fulfillment in Joshua
- **Passages:** Joshua 1:1-9, Joshua 21:43-45
- **Key Terms:** Land promise, covenant faithfulness

### Lesson 16: Cycle of Judges
- **Objective:** Recognize covenant faithfulness cycle in Judges
- **Passages:** Judges 2:11-19
- **Key Terms:** Apostasy, judges

### Lesson 17: Establishment of Kingship
- **Objective:** Understand the transition to monarchy in Israel
- **Passages:** 1 Samuel 8:4-9, 1 Samuel 10:17-25
- **Key Terms:** Kingship, theocracy

### Lesson 18: Davidic Covenant
- **Objective:** Identify key elements of the Davidic covenant
- **Passages:** 2 Samuel 7:8-16, Psalm 89:3-4
- **Key Terms:** Davidic covenant, messianic promise

### Lesson 19: Temple and Centralization
- **Objective:** Explain Solomon's temple in covenant theology
- **Passages:** 1 Kings 8:22-30, 1 Kings 8:54-61
- **Key Terms:** Temple, centralized worship

### Lesson 20: Prophetic Covenant Critique
- **Objective:** Recognize prophetic enforcement of covenant obligations
- **Passages:** Amos 5:21-24, Micah 6:6-8
- **Key Terms:** Prophetic literature, covenant lawsuit

---

## Unit 5: Exile and Restoration (4 lessons)

### Lesson 21: Covenant Curse and Exile
- **Objective:** Trace covenant disobedience to exile consequence
- **Passages:** 2 Kings 17:7-18, Jeremiah 25:8-11
- **Key Terms:** Exile, covenant curse

### Lesson 22: Prophetic Hope
- **Objective:** Identify restoration promises in prophetic literature
- **Passages:** Jeremiah 31:31-34, Ezekiel 36:24-28
- **Key Terms:** New covenant, restoration

### Lesson 23: Return and Rebuilding
- **Objective:** Describe post-exilic restoration efforts
- **Passages:** Ezra 3:1-6, Nehemiah 8:1-8
- **Key Terms:** Post-exilic period, second temple

### Lesson 24: Waiting and Expectation
- **Objective:** Explain intertestamental hope for covenant fulfillment
- **Passages:** Malachi 3:1-4, Malachi 4:5-6
- **Key Terms:** Messianic expectation, intertestamental period

---

## Course Completion Assessment

### Final Knowledge Check (Multiple Choice)
1. What are the three elements of the Abrahamic covenant promise?
2. What literary form does the Sinai covenant follow?
3. What is the primary function of the tabernacle in Exodus?
4. What dynasty does the Davidic covenant establish?
5. What does Jeremiah 31 promise regarding a new covenant?

### Completion Certificate
Upon finishing all 24 lessons and passing the final assessment, students receive:
- Course completion notation
- Unlocking of Biblical Foundations II (if available)
- Progress recorded in user profile
